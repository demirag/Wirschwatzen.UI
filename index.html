§ 01 · The Wall

Shipping a voice agent is easy. Proving it's ready isn't.

Manual QA is slow, expensive, and impossible to scale. Edge cases slip through. Production failures arrive as customer complaints. We've spent ten years hitting this wall — Wir_Schwatzen is what we built to break it.

— Wall · I

Manual QA
takes weeks.

A native speaker, a headset, a spreadsheet. The same forty calls every Friday. Most don't break — and the ones that do never get retested.

— Wall · II

Edge cases
slip through.

Swabian dialect. Toddler in the background. The caller who says "actually, no" mid-sentence. The cases you never tested fail loudest in production.

— Wall · III

Failures arrive
as complaints.

Senior engineer reproduces it on Friday afternoon. Hotfix Sunday morning. The damage was already done — the question is only how much.

§ 06 · Sovereignty

Where your voices live.

Every byte of audio, every transcript, every voice print stays inside European Union infrastructure. Encrypted in transit, encrypted at rest, deletable on demand.

▸ note · 01

EU-only by construction.

Hosted in Frankfurt am Main on infrastructure owned and operated within the EEA. No US-resident sub-processors touch your call audio. Ever.

▸ note · 02

Bring-your-own keys.

Customer-managed encryption keys via HSM. Revoke at any time; revocation is auditable and renders historical evidence unreadable — including to us.

▸ note · 03

Audit-ready, by default.

Every verdict carries an immutable evidence packet: audio, transcript, prompt snapshot, model fingerprint, timestamps. One click for BaFin or your DPO.

▸ Filed in Nürnberg · 13 May 2026 · § 07

Building voice agents is solved. Proving they're ready isn't.

Our team has spent the last decade at the front of conversational AI. We were in the room when this technology was being built from raw transformers and audio buffers; we shipped production voice agents long before "voice agent" was a category.

Through every release we kept running into the same wall: evaluation. No professional standard existed. No European-first tooling. Just slow, manual processes that could never keep up with weekly model swaps and prompt revisions.

So we built what we wished had existed — in Nürnberg, in 2026, for the agents Europe is about to deploy by the million.

The bar for "production-ready" cannot be a Friday-afternoon spot check in English. Not anymore. Not here.
